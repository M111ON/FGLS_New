The GeoPixel v8 encoder follows a sophisticated pipeline designed to remove redundancy at every level (color, space,
  and bit-representation) before finally handing the data to ZSTD.

  Here is the step-by-step flow of how a raw image becomes a .gp8 file:

  1. Reversible Color Transform (YCgCo-R)
  Instead of storing Red, Green, and Blue, which are highly correlated (if a pixel is bright red, it's often bright
  green too), we convert them to a "Luminance/Chrominance" space:
   * Y (Luminance): The brightness.
   * Cg/Co (Chrominance): The difference between colors.
   * Why? This "decorrelates" the channels. The Y channel contains the detail, while Cg/Co channels become very "flat"
     and easy to compress. We use the -R (Reversible) version to ensure zero data loss during the math.

  2. Tiled 2D Spatial Prediction (8x8 Tiles)
  We divide the image into small 8x8 tiles. For each tile, the encoder "auditions" 4 different mathematical predictors:
   1. MED (Median Edge Detection): Good for sharp edges.
   2. AVG: Smooths out gradients.
   3. GRAD: Follows complex slopes.
   4. LEFT: Simple horizontal copy.
   * The Decision: The encoder runs all 4 on the Y channel and picks the one that results in the smallest residuals
     (errors). It saves this "winner ID" into the Predictor Map.

  3. Residual Calculation
  Once a predictor is chosen for a tile, the encoder calculates the "Residual" for every pixel:
   * Residual = Actual_Value - Predicted_Value
   * If the predictor is good, the residual will be 0 or a very small number (like -1 or +1).

  4. Interleaving & ZigZag Mapping
  This is where v8 differs most from v7:
   * Interleaving: We pack the residuals as [Y, Cg, Co, Y, Cg, Co...]. Because Y, Cg, and Co are physically related,
     ZSTD can find patterns across the three channels simultaneously.
   * ZigZag Mapping: Computers store signed numbers (like -1) in a way that looks "messy" to compressors. ZigZag maps
     them:
       * 0 → 0, -1 → 1, 1 → 2, -2 → 3, 2 → 4...
       * This turns all small errors into small positive integers, which creates long strings of zeros and small numbers
         that ZSTD loves.

  5. Multi-Threaded ZSTD Entropy Coding
  Finally, the stream of ZigZag-mapped residuals and the Predictor Map are sent to the ZSTD engine:
   * Multi-threading: The encoder uses ZSTD_compressStream2 to split the massive data stream into chunks.
   * Parallel Search: On your dual-core CPU, ZSTD searches for repeating patterns in these chunks using both cores at
     once.
   * Level 19 vs 3: At high levels, it builds a massive "dictionary" of patterns to squeeze every last bit.

  6. File Packing
  The encoder writes the .gp8 header (Width, Height, Tile counts, and Stream sizes) followed by the compressed ZSTD
  blobs.

  ---

  Summary of the "Magic"
   1. YCgCo removes color redundancy.
   2. Predictors remove spatial redundancy (detail).
   3. ZigZag prepares the data for the compressor.
   4. ZSTD MT removes the final bit-level redundancy using all your CPU power.
